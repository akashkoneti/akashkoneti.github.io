Statistical & Analytical Methods in Proteomics

Normalization

Normalization methods are applied to make samples comparable and remove batch effects.

Identifying Differentially Expressed Proteins

After preprocessing, statistical tests are used to find proteins with significant changes in abundance between different experimental groups (e.g., diseased vs. healthy).

  • T-tests: Compare protein abundance between two groups. Moderated t-tests (e.g., limma) borrow information across proteins for stable variance estimates.
  • ANOVA: Extends t-test for more than two groups or multi-factor experiments (e.g., time-course or dose-response).
  • Fold Change Analysis: Measures ratio of protein abundance between two conditions. Should be interpreted alongside statistical significance.
  • Volcano Plots: Combine fold change and p-values to highlight potential differentially expressed proteins.
  • Multiple Testing Correction: Controls statistical errors when testing thousands of proteins simultaneously.
Multivariate Analysis
  • Principal Component Analysis (PCA): Reduces dimensionality while preserving major variation, useful for clustering, outlier detection, and batch effect identification.
  • Clustering: Hierarchical or k-means clustering groups proteins or samples with similar patterns.
  • Machine Learning: Supervised and unsupervised methods (Random Forest, SVM, Elastic Net) help with feature selection and predictive modeling.
False Discovery Rate (FDR) & Q-value
  • FDR: Controls the expected proportion of false positives among significant findings. Benjamini-Hochberg is a common method.
  • Q-value: FDR-adjusted p-value reflecting the minimum FDR at which a protein can be considered significant.

Technical notes

MaxQuant and Proteome Discoverer exports are supported alongside generic CSV/TSV schemas. Preprocessing options include standard scaling, variance stabilizing normalization, and multiple imputation strategies selectable inside the Shiny UI.

Outputs can be exported as CSV/TSV, PDF/PNG figures, and reproducible HTML reports. Parameter settings are preserved to allow re-running analyses with the same pipeline.
